FAQs

Frequently asked questions

Q1.What is medical billing and coding?

Medical billing is the process of submitting claims to insurance companies for reimbursement, while coding involves translating patient diagnoses and treatments into standardized codes for billing and record-keeping purposes.

Q2.How do errors in medical coding affect billing?

Errors in medical coding can lead to claim denials or delays in payment, resulting in lost revenue for healthcare providers. Accurate coding ensures timely and full reimbursement.

Q3.What is the difference between ICD-10 and CPT codes?

ICD-10 codes are used for diagnosing conditions, while CPT codes describe the medical procedures performed. Both are necessary for processing healthcare claims.

Q4.How long does it take to get reimbursed after a claim is submitted?

Reimbursement timelines vary, but it typically takes 30-45 days from claim submission to receive payment, depending on the payer's process and whether there are any issues with the claim.

Q5.What role does insurance verification play in the billing process?

Insurance verification ensures that a patient’s coverage is active and determines what services are covered, reducing the likelihood of denied claims and payment delays.
